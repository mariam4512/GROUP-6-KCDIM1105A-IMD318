Understanding Combination Skin

Characteristics

  • Oily T-zone (forehead, nose, chin)
  • Dry cheeks and jawline
  • Different pore sizes in different areas
  • Seasonal changes in skin behavior

What Combination Skin Looks Like

Real example of combination skin condition

Common Signs to Look For:

  • Mixed Texture:

    Oily T-zone with drier cheeks and jawline

  • Variable Pore Size:

    Larger pores in oily areas, finer in dry areas

  • Seasonal Changes:

    Skin conditions vary with weather and seasons

If your skin shows similar signs, follow our recommended care routine below.

24-Hour Care Routine

7:00 AM - Morning Routine
  • • Gentle balancing cleanser
  • • Hydrating toner
  • • Light serum with niacinamide
  • • Zone-specific moisturizer application
  • • Oil-free sunscreen
2:00 PM - Midday Care
  • • Selective blotting (T-zone only)
  • • Hydrating mist for dry areas
  • • Touch-up sunscreen as needed
9:00 PM - Evening Routine
  • • Double cleanse method
  • • Balance-restoring toner
  • • Different serums for different zones
  • • Lightweight gel for T-zone
  • • Rich cream for dry areas
  • • Multi-masking weekly (clay for T-zone, hydrating for cheeks)

Special Considerations

Seasonal Changes

  • Summer: Focus on oil control in T-zone, maintain hydration in dry areas
  • Winter: Increase overall hydration while maintaining balance

Multi-Masking Guide

T-Zone Treatment

Apply clay or charcoal mask to control oil and minimize pores

Cheek Areas

Use hydrating or cream mask to nourish and moisturize

Pro Tips for Combination Skin

Layer Products Wisely

Apply lighter products all over, then add richer products only on dry areas

Temperature Matters

Use lukewarm water for cleansing to avoid triggering excess oil production

Timing is Key

Adjust your routine based on seasonal changes and skin's current needs
